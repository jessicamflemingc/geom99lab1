Hawaiian Islands

The Hawaiian Islands were formed by volcanic activity and this map explores the locations of the volcanoes that are responsilbe for the creation of the islands. Hawaii is an example of island chanins created by hot-spot volcanism. Each island is successively older toward the northwest. The purple markers indiciate active volcanoes, the green are inactive (or dormant) volcanoes and the yellow signifies the extninct volcanoes
The active volcanoes consist of Mauna Kea, Hualalai, Mauna Loa, Kilauea, Haleakala and the kama'ehuakanaloa Seamount (formerly known as the Lo'ihi Seamount).
